# Usage Guide - The Attention Receipt

## Quick Start Demo

### 1. Load Demo Data (Fastest Way to See It in Action)
- Click **"Load Demo Data"** button
- Or press **Ctrl+K** (Cmd+K on Mac)
- Instantly see a receipt for a 14-year-old with 3h 50m screen time

### 2. Create Your Own Receipt

**Step 1: Set Age**
- Enter child's age (0-18 years)
- Age determines risk assessment framework

**Step 2: Add Apps**
- Enter app name (e.g., "TikTok", "YouTube", "Fortnite")
- Select category:
  - **Social Media** - TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat
  - **Video/Streaming** - YouTube, Netflix, Disney+
  - **Gaming** - Minecraft, Fortnite, Roblox
  - **Educational/School** - Canvas, Khan Academy, Duolingo
  - **Other** - Everything else

- Enter time spent (hours and minutes)
- Click **"Add App"**

**Step 3: Review Receipt**
- Real-time receipt updates on right side
- Shows total attention spent
- Displays age-specific risk assessment
- Provides actionable swap suggestion

### 3. Share Your Receipt

**Download as Image**
- Click **"Download as PNG"** (or Ctrl+D)
- High-quality image for printing or sharing
- Great for doctor visits, parent-teacher conferences

**Share via Link**
- Click **"Copy Shareable Link"**
- Paste anywhere - the URL contains all receipt data
- Others can view and modify your receipt
- No account or login required

## Understanding Risk Assessment

### Risk Levels Explained

| Icon | Level | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| ✓ | LOW | Within healthy limits for this age |
| △ | MODERATE | Approaching concerning levels |
| ⚠ | ELEVATED | Significantly increased risk |
| ⚠⚠ | HIGH | Strongly elevated risk |

### Age-Specific Risk Factors

**Ages 0-5**
- **Primary concern:** ADHD
- Screen time in early childhood shows strongest association with attention problems
- Even moderate use (2+ hours) raises risk

**Ages 6-11**
- **Balanced risk** across mental health and physical activity
- Physical activity risk increases significantly at 4+ hours/day
- Building healthy habits is critical in this window

**Ages 12-17**
- **Primary concern:** Anxiety & Depression
- Social media time weighted heavily in assessment
- **3+ hours social media = 2.1× higher anxiety odds** (direct from research)
- This is the most vulnerable age group for mental health impacts

**Ages 18+**
- More moderate assessment (research focused on children)
- Physical activity remains a concern

## Practical Tips

### Accurate Time Tracking

**Where to Find Screen Time Data:**
- **iPhone/iPad:** Settings → Screen Time → See All Activity
- **Android:** Settings → Digital Wellbeing & Parental Controls
- **Windows:** Settings → System → Storage → App usage
- **Mac:** Screen Time in System Preferences
- **Gaming consoles:** Check parental controls dashboard

**Pro Tip:** Export weekly reports from device settings, then aggregate into daily average

### Making Effective Swaps

The tool provides swap suggestions, but here's how to implement them:

1. **Start Small:** Don't try to swap everything at once
2. **Replace, Don't Remove:** Swap TikTok time with basketball, not empty time
3. **Same Reward Different Activity:** If gaming = stress relief, try exercise instead
4. **Track Progress:** Create new receipt weekly to see improvement

### Category Guidelines

**When to use "School" category:**
- Canvas, Google Classroom (course management)
- Khan Academy, Duolingo (educational apps)
- Zoom for remote class (NOT social Zoom calls)

**Social vs Video:**
- If content is algorithm-driven and endless scroll → Social
- If content is chosen/intentional watching → Video
- TikTok/Instagram Reels → Social
- YouTube educational videos → Video

## FAQs

**Q: Is my data saved anywhere?**
A: No. Everything happens in your browser. The only "storage" is optional shareable links (data encoded in URL).

**Q: Can I track multiple children?**
A: Create separate receipts for each child. Use the shareable link feature to keep multiple receipts organized.

**Q: How accurate are the risk assessments?**
A: Based on 315,000+ child observations from NSCH 2018-2024. However, individual circumstances vary - use this as a discussion tool, not a diagnostic.

**Q: What about educational screen time?**
A: The research limitation is that SCREENTIME can't distinguish content type. That's why this tool lets you categorize. However, even "educational" content can displace physical activity.

**Q: My child uses devices for school. Should I count that?**
A: Yes, but categorize it as "School". The receipt shows the breakdown so you can see school vs recreational split.

**Q: Can I print the receipt?**
A: Yes! Download as PNG, then print. The thermal receipt design actually looks great on paper.

## Best Practices

### Do's
✅ Use as a **conversation tool**, not punishment
✅ Create receipts **together** with your child
✅ Focus on **swap suggestions** rather than just restriction
✅ Track **trends over time** (weekly receipts)
✅ Share with healthcare providers for evidence-based discussions

### Don'ts
❌ Don't use as a shaming tool
❌ Don't treat educational screen time and social media equally
❌ Don't rely solely on this tool - it's one data point
❌ Don't expect perfection - awareness is the first step
❌ Don't ignore context (e.g., staying connected with long-distance family)
